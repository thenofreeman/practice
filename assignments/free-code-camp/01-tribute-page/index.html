Christopher Nolan

The Worlds Greatest Director

A timeline of Christopher Nolan's film career:

  • The Following - A 1998 British neo-noir crime thriller film. It tells the story of a young man who follows strangers around the streets of London and is drawn into a criminal underworld when he fails to keep his distance.
  • Momento - The 2000 film follows a man searching for the persons who attacked him and killed his wife, using an intricate system of Polaroid photographs and tattoos to track information he cannot remember.
  • Insomnia - 2002, Starring Al Pacino, Robin Williams, and Hilary Swank. It tells the story of two Los Angeles homicide detectives investigating a murder in an Alaskan town.
  • Batman Begins - The 2005 film reboots the Batman film series, telling the origin story of Bruce Wayne from the death of his parents to his journey to become Batman and his fight to stop Ra's al Ghul and the Scarecrow from plunging Gotham City into chaos.
  • The Prestige - This story follows Robert Angier and Alfred Borden, rival stage magicians in London at the end of the 19th century. Obsessed with creating the best stage illusion, they engage in competitive one-upmanship, with tragic results. (released 2006)
  • The Dark Knight - In the 2008, second of three films, Bruce Wayne / Batman, Police Lieutenant James Gordon (Oldman) and District Attorney Harvey Dent (Eckhart) form an alliance to dismantle organized crime in Gotham City, but are menaced by an anarchistic mastermind known as the Joker (Ledger), who seeks to undermine Batman's influence and turn the city to chaos.
  • Inception - The 2010 film stars Leonardo DiCaprio as a professional thief who steals information by infiltrating the subconscious, and is offered a chance to have his criminal history erased as payment for the implantation of another person's idea into a target's subconscious.
  • The Dark Knight Rises - Eight years after the events of The Dark Knight, merciless revolutionary Bane forces an older Bruce Wayne to resume his role as Batman and save Gotham City from nuclear destruction. (2012)
  • Interstellar - AKA The greatest film of all time; Set in a dystopian future where humanity is struggling to survive, the film follows a group of astronauts who travel through a wormhole near Saturn in search of a new home for humanity. (2014)
  • Dunkirk - Dunkirk portrays the evacuation of WWII from three perspectives: land, sea, and air. It has little dialogue, as Nolan sought instead to create suspense from cinematography and music. (2017)
  • Tenet - Is an upcoming 2020 action thriller. Not much is known about the film other than its July 2020 release date and that it will be amazing.
